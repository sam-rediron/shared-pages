AP Automation Evaluation Framework

How we're comparing platforms — the criteria, what they measure, and why each one matters for Charter Impact's specific environment. This framework will be used to score each platform during and after vendor demos.

Platforms Under Evaluation

What's Being Compared

Six platforms are included in the evaluation. Three are being evaluated as new additions to Charter Impact's stack. Three are current systems being assessed against the same criteria for an objective, apples-to-apples comparison — so the reasoning behind any changes is documented and visible.

BILL Stampli Basis Edstruments Procurify Sage Intacct
Evaluating for adoption Current system — benchmarked GL baseline — not changing
Methodology

How Scoring Will Work

Each criterion below is tagged Critical, Important, or Nice to Have based on the pain points and requirements surfaced during AP team discovery and leadership conversations. Criteria are organized into two tiers.

Tier 1 — AP Automation Requirements covers the capabilities that solve the problems this initiative was organized around. These are the criteria where the current systems fall short and where the evaluation will be won or lost.

Tier 2 — Strategic & Business Criteria covers the dimensions where existing partnerships, business relationships, adoption feasibility, and cost matter. This is where Procurify and Edstruments earn their points — and where trade-offs between operational capability and business value become visible.

During demos, each platform will be scored against every applicable criterion. Categories will be weighted so the final comparison reflects Charter Impact's priorities. The alignment session before demos is where we agree on those weights together.

Tier 1 — AP Automation Requirements The problems this initiative was built to solve
01 Invoice Coding Intelligence & Verification
Charter Impact's core complexity: every invoice must be manually coded to the correct GL account, location, resource code, and LCAP function — and every client has a different chart of accounts. Sage's built-in OCR regularly misreads invoice numbers, amounts, vendor names, and credit memos. 51 coding errors were found in a single client review. This category measures how much of that manual verification burden the platform can absorb.
Critical
Coding intelligence approach
What method does the platform use to suggest GL codes? Basic OCR only, pattern-based ML that learns from history, or contextual AI that reasons about invoice content, vendor context, and client rules? Pattern-based ML works for consistent coding but struggles when the same vendor gets coded differently by client, funding source, or month.
Critical
Preset coding rules / templates (string lines)
Can the platform pre-fill GL coding fields (object code, function code, resource code) per vendor or per vendor + client combination? Charter Impact's prior system (MIP) had "string lines" that did this — Sage lost this capability. Can rules include conditional logic (e.g., Vendor X codes to supplies normally, but services when the line item is a repair)?
Critical
Multi-line invoice splits
Can the platform handle invoices that need splitting into multiple GL lines? Workers' comp (premium vs. components), copier leases (taxes, overages, base lease), employee reimbursements (supplies, mileage, meals). Does it support templates for recurring split patterns? Does it validate that line totals match the invoice total?
Critical
Credit memo handling
Does the platform structurally prevent credit memos from being processed as debits? Sage currently pushes credit memos through as invoices due — if AP doesn't catch it, the credit gets paid out. Does the platform auto-detect credit memos from the document, or require manual designation?
Critical
New line item / coding deviation detection
When a known vendor changes what it provides (a supplies vendor adds a service line item), does the platform flag the deviation or silently default to historical coding? The AP team needs visibility into when the AI is uncertain, not just when it's confident.
Important
Confidence scoring
Does the platform provide per-field (not just per-invoice) confidence indicators? Can the team configure a threshold above which items auto-advance and below which they're flagged for review? None of Charter Impact's current systems flag when the AI is uncertain about an extracted field.
Important
Past-due balance parsing on utility invoices
Utility invoices show past-due balances alongside current charges. Sage captures the full amount. Charter Impact's policy is to only process current-period charges. Can the platform distinguish and separate these automatically when the invoice shows distinct line items?
Important
Batch PDF splitting
High-volume schools submit batch PDFs with 50–120+ pages containing multiple invoices and varying-length backup documents. Can the platform auto-detect invoice boundaries, or does it require manual boundary selection? How does this compare to the current workflow of manually splitting in a PDF tool and re-uploading?
Important
Invoice data extraction accuracy
How accurately does the platform read vendor names, invoice numbers (especially alphanumeric — Amazon invoices are the worst offender), amounts (commas vs. decimals, subtotals vs. totals), and dates? What is the stated field extraction accuracy rate? Amazon invoices alone account for 2,000–3,000 per week.
02 Payment Selection & Execution
The payment selection bottleneck is the single biggest time sink in AP operations. Clients select which vendors to pay by marking up an aging report in Excel. The associate then manually translates those selections into Sage's Pay Bills screen, one invoice at a time with a full page reload between each action. For high-volume clients this consumes 6+ hours per week. One client takes nearly a full day.
Critical
Vendor-level payment selection
Charter Impact's clients don't approve individual invoices — they select which vendors to pay based on cash availability. Can the platform group invoices by vendor in the payment queue? Can the team bulk-select all invoices for a vendor? Can they work in both directions: "pay all except these vendors" and "pay only these vendors"?
Critical
Bulk payment processing
Can the platform process payments in bulk (hundreds at a time) without per-transaction clicks? Procurify currently allows only one payment at a time. Edstruments caps at 50. Sage requires one-by-one selection with page reloads. What are the batch limits?
Critical
Payment methods
Does the platform support ACH, printed checks, and virtual cards? Do individual transactions appear separately on bank statements for easy reconciliation? What are per-transaction costs for each method?
Critical
Rush / same-day payments
Charter Impact handles rush and same-day payments in-house today because no third-party platform has met their speed requirements. Does the platform support same-day ACH? Expedited checks — and if so, what's the delivery window? If the platform can't handle same-day physical checks, Charter Impact needs to retain an in-house path for that subset.
Critical
Bidirectional payment sync with Sage
Does payment status flow back to Sage automatically? Edstruments has no payment status sync-back — associates manually update it after every payment run. Procurify's sync is partial. This manual sync-back is pure overhead. Automatic bidirectional sync eliminates it.
Important
Payment status visibility for clients
Clients want more transparency into when checks are cut, sent, and received. Does the platform provide client-facing payment status tracking or notifications?
Important
Credit application across entities
Some clients have credits recorded under one location that need to be applied to bills at multiple locations. Can the platform facilitate cross-entity credit application, or is this constrained by how Sage handles intercompany transactions?
Nice to Have
Electronic / email checks
A check delivered digitally for the vendor to print and deposit — reducing physical check printing volume and delivery logistics while accommodating vendors that require check payments.
Nice to Have
Positive pay support
Does the platform generate positive pay files for bank upload, or does that remain a manual step in the current Sage workflow?
03 Sage Intacct Integration Depth
Sage Intacct is the GL and it's not changing. Every platform must work as an overlay on Sage. The depth of that integration — sync direction, frequency, dimension awareness, and whether Sage's own business logic fires on transactions made through the platform — directly affects coding accuracy and operational reliability across 100+ client entities.
Critical
Sync direction and frequency
Is the sync bidirectional? What's the frequency — real-time, every 5 minutes, daily, on-demand? When a client adds a new location or changes an object code in Sage mid-cycle, how quickly does the AP platform reflect that change? Stampli confirms 5-minute dimension sync. BILL offers daily auto + on-demand manual.
Critical
Dimension-aware sync
Does the platform sync Sage's full dimension model — locations, departments, classes, projects, custom dimensions? Charter Impact's clients use complex dimension structures. If the platform doesn't sync dimensions, coding in the AP layer won't match what Sage expects.
Critical
Smart Rules triggering
Sage's Smart Rules automate coding behavior (e.g., selecting a department auto-fills the related fund). Do transactions created through the AP platform trigger Smart Rules in Sage, or does the AP layer bypass them? Bypassing means losing a validation layer the team currently relies on.
Critical
Multi-entity management at scale
Can Charter Impact operate 100+ client entities from a single operational team within the platform? Most AP tools are built for one company managing its own books. Charter Impact needs multi-client, multi-entity complexity managed as a service on behalf of clients.
Important
Custom object support
Does the platform support Sage custom objects and custom fields beyond the standard dimension model? BILL claims support for up to 6 custom objects. Charter school-specific fields (LCAP functions, resource codes, funding source tracking) may require this.
Important
Entity-based user restrictions
Can the platform enforce Sage's entity-based access controls — ensuring an associate or client user can only see and act on their assigned entities? This is a compliance and data isolation requirement for a multi-client services firm.
Important
Sage ecosystem standing
Is the platform a Sage Recommended Solution, listed on the Sage Marketplace, or neither? Sage Recommended status signals deeper integration testing and ongoing compatibility. Stampli is Sage Recommended. Procurify is Marketplace-listed. BILL is Marketplace-listed.
04 Procurement & Approval Workflows
The reason Charter Impact's clients are on Edstruments and Procurify in the first place: Sage only supports amount-based approval thresholds. Clients need department-based routing, location-based routing, and multi-level approval chains. If an AP platform can satisfy these same needs, it opens the door to consolidating clients off multiple systems. If it can't, the multi-system fragmentation persists — but the AP platform still handles coding and payments downstream.
Critical
Approval workflow depth
Does the platform support department-based routing, amount-based multi-level approvals, location/role-based routing, and combined routing rules (department + amount + location)? This is the specific capability gap in Sage that drives clients to Edstruments and Procurify.
Critical
Bulk client-side invoice approval
Can a client approve 200+ invoices at once? Sage's built-in bulk approval caps at 30, which is why high-volume schools use watermarked batch PDFs instead. A higher limit could change client approval behavior entirely, eliminating the batch PDF workflow.
Important
Client-facing approval interface
How do clients interact with the approval workflow? In-app, email link, mobile? Charter Impact's clients currently approve through physical watermarks/stamps (ShareFile), electronic approval in Sage, or approval within Edstruments/Procurify. A new system needs to match or exceed the ease of the current methods.
Important
PO matching
Does the platform support matching invoices against purchase orders? Two-way (PO to invoice) or three-way (PO to receipt to invoice)? Procurify offers full three-way matching. This matters for clients with formal procurement processes.
Important
PO punch-outs / supplier catalog integrations
Does the platform integrate directly with supplier catalogs (Amazon Business, Staples, Home Depot, Grainger, Uline)? This is Procurify's key differentiator and the primary feature preventing some clients from migrating off Procurify. AP automation platforms typically don't offer this.
05 Analytics, Reporting & Management Visibility
Charter Impact's leadership needs specific operational metrics to manage the AP team effectively. Currently, aggregate team volume can be reported but not broken down by individual — Sage doesn't support this. Compiling void/reissue data takes 2–4 hours of manual data gathering to produce a single number. General leadership meeting prep takes 3–4 hours.
Important
Per-associate invoice throughput
Can the platform report how many invoices each associate processes per week? Charter Impact's previous system had this; Sage doesn't. Current tracking relies on a manual spreadsheet with self-reported weekly counts that can't be independently verified.
Important
Void/reissue tracking
Can the platform track voids and reissues with structured data — reason codes, dates, amounts — rather than requiring narrative manual compilation? Can custom fields or tags be added to records (e.g., "error type" on a void)?
Important
Operational dashboards
Does the platform provide a real-time view of processing status, exception rates, processing times, and workload balance — replacing the manual tracking spreadsheet and verbal check-ins at the daily standup?
Nice to Have
Leadership-ready reporting
Can reports be exported or scheduled in a format ready for leadership review without hours of manual reformatting? Does the platform support the operational metrics Charter Impact's leadership regularly needs?
06 Invoice Intake & Submission
Invoices enter Charter Impact's workflow from multiple channels: ShareFile drop folders, direct upload into procurement systems, email, and (for some) directly into Sage. Any new platform needs to connect to these existing intake paths — or provide a compelling enough alternative that clients are willing to change behavior.
Important
Intake methods supported
Can invoices be submitted via email, direct upload, vendor portal, and/or integration with existing file exchange systems (ShareFile, SharePoint)? Does the platform provide a dedicated AP email address that auto-ingests attachments?
Important
Upload capacity and limits
Sage is limited to ~20–30 files at a time with a 20MB cap per batch. What are the platform's upload limits? Can it handle bulk uploads of 50+ files without failures or manual retries?
Nice to Have
Compatibility with existing client systems
For clients who stay on other systems (e.g., Ramp), can invoices processed in those systems still flow through to the new AP platform for coding and payment? The new system needs to work in tandem with systems clients already use, not require full replacement across the board.
Tier 2 — Strategic & Business Criteria Business context, partnerships, costs, and adoption
07 Vendor Profile & Durability
Charter Impact needs confidence that the vendor will be around in 3–5 years and that the platform will continue to be maintained and improved. This category assesses company fundamentals, ecosystem position, and implementation support.
Important
Company maturity and track record
How long has the company been operating? Customer base size? Funding history and financial stability? A newer platform with $100M in funding (Basis, founded 2023) carries different risk than a publicly traded company (BILL) or an established mid-market player (Stampli).
Important
Implementation and onboarding support
What does the implementation process look like? Dedicated onboarding team? Timeline? How hands-on is vendor support during rollout versus being one of thousands of customers? Charter Impact's AP team is currently short-staffed — capacity for piloting is limited.
Important
Security and compliance
SOC 2 compliance? ISO certifications? Data handling practices? Charter Impact handles financial data for 100+ client organizations — security standards are non-negotiable.
Nice to Have
API availability and documentation
Is there a public, documented API for building custom integrations or automations? This affects long-term flexibility. Basis's API is not yet publicly documented, which limits integration options today.
08 Strategic & Business Fit
The decision isn't just operational — it has business relationship and revenue implications. Procurify brings in significant deal flow. Edstruments is a referral partner. BILL has an existing Red Iron Group relationship. These dimensions need to be weighed alongside operational capability so the trade-offs are visible and deliberate.
Critical
Partnership and lead generation value
Does this platform drive revenue for Charter Impact through referrals, co-selling, or sales partnerships? Procurify's partnership has driven significant deal flow. Any system change that makes Procurify redundant for some clients needs to be weighed against that partnership's value. The goal is optionality — offering the right system per client — not forced consolidation.
Important
Red Iron Group relationship and leverage
Is the vendor already used by other RIG portfolio companies? BILL is used by two other portfolio companies, and a firm CEO sits on BILL's advisory board — providing pricing leverage and implementation support. This kind of relationship advantage matters for negotiation and accountability.
Important
System consolidation potential
Could this platform replace Edstruments and/or Procurify for some or all clients? Fewer systems means less training burden, less context-switching, no manual payment-status sync-back, and a simpler foundation for future automation. But consolidation that disrupts valuable partnerships needs to justify itself with clear operational ROI.
Important
Education-specific capabilities
Is the platform built for or experienced with K-12 charter school environments? Does it natively understand LCAP functions, resource codes, funding source tracking, ADA-based allocation splits? Edstruments scores highest here — it's K-12 only. General-purpose platforms need to handle these via configuration.
Important
Adoption feasibility
What's the training burden? How much change management is needed? Can the team absorb a new system alongside daily workload? Current staffing constraints affect near-term capacity for piloting. One new system is easier to adopt than two.
09 Cost & Total Cost of Ownership
All platforms use enterprise pricing. Costs will be evaluated during and after demos. The question is whether the operational savings and risk reduction justify the investment — and whether volume negotiation at the Red Iron Group level improves the economics.
Critical
Licensing model and per-entity cost
How is the platform priced — per user, per entity, per transaction, flat fee? At 100+ client entities, per-entity pricing compounds quickly. What's the estimated annual cost at Charter Impact's scale?
Important
Per-transaction fees
What are ACH fees, check fees, and virtual card fees per transaction? Current cost for ACH via NACHA file upload is essentially free (5–10 minutes of associate time). A quoted ~$1/transaction for ACH previously made outsourcing uneconomical. What's the breakeven?
Important
Implementation and setup costs
One-time implementation fees, data migration costs, training costs, and integration setup. What's the total first-year investment versus ongoing annual cost?
Important
ROI and payback period
How quickly does the operational savings (reduced manual hours, fewer errors, capacity to serve more clients without proportional headcount) pay back the investment? Payment operations savings are immediate and quantifiable. Coding accuracy improvement is harder to quantify but addresses higher-stakes risk.
Nice to Have
1099 filing and EIN validation
Does the platform include 1099 filing capabilities with EIN validation against IRS records? Charter Impact currently uses a costly third-party service. The 2026 IRS threshold change ($600 → $2,000) reduces 1099 volume by ~25%. This conversation needs to happen by approximately October.
Nice to Have
Vendor ID inconsistency handling
No AP layer can fix Sage's underlying vendor records (names/IDs can't be changed once set). But does the platform offer normalized display, EIN-based grouping, or better filtering that mitigates the sort-order and searchability problems caused by inconsistent naming conventions?

This framework will be used to structure vendor demos and score each platform. Category weights will be set during the alignment session before demos begin.
