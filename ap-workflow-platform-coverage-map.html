Charter Impact — AP Workflow & Platform Coverage Map

Prepared: May 2026 For: Charter Impact Leadership (Adam, David, Vireak) From: RIG Builders (Sam, Jason)
Charter Impact's AP workflow breaks into six stages. Every invoice passes through some or all of these stages on its way from submission to the general ledger. The platforms Charter Impact uses today — and the platforms being evaluated — each cover different stages of this workflow. This document maps that coverage so everyone can see, at a glance, where each system plays, where the gaps are, and where the pain points live.

The Six Stages of AP at Charter Impact

Stage 1
Invoice Submission

How invoices enter the system. Clients submit via ShareFile drop folders, direct upload into a procurement system, email, or directly into Sage. High-volume schools often submit batch PDFs containing 50–120+ pages that must be manually split before processing.

Stage 2
Procurement & Approval

Client-facing approval workflows — department-based routing, amount thresholds, multi-level approval chains, PO matching. This is the stage that drives clients to Edstruments and Procurify, because Sage only supports amount-based thresholds.

Stage 3 — Pain Point
Invoice Coding & Verification

Every invoice is coded to the correct GL account, location, resource code, LCAP function, and memo — and every client has different rules. Sage's OCR regularly misreads invoice numbers, amounts, vendor names, and credit memos. This is where coding intelligence matters most.

Stage 4 — Pain Point
Payment Selection

Clients review an aging report and select which vendors to pay. Today this happens via Excel markup by email. The associate manually re-enters selections into Sage one by one, with a full page reload between each action. For high-volume clients: 6+ hours/week of purely mechanical work.

Stage 5
Payment Execution

Check runs, ACH/NACHA file generation, positive pay uploads, and payment distribution. Currently handled in Sage with manual handoffs to analysts. Same-day and rush payments are handled in-house because no third-party platform has met CI's requirements.

Stage 6
GL Sync & Accounting

Transactions land in Sage Intacct and flow into month-end close — reconciliation, journal entries, financial statement packages. The quality of everything upstream determines how much manual cleanup happens here.

What the Map Reveals

Where the pain is — and what covers it

Charter Impact's two biggest AP pain points are at Stage 3 (manual invoice coding across highly variable clients) and Stage 4 (the payment selection bottleneck). The current systems handle Stages 1–2 well but barely touch Stages 3–5.

Edstruments & Procurify

Built to solve the procurement and approval problem (Stage 2) — and they do that effectively. But they offer only basic OCR at Stage 3, limited payment selection at Stage 4 (Edstruments allows multi-select of 50 at a time; Procurify only allows one payment at a time), and limited or problematic payment execution at Stage 5. Neither supports vendor-level bulk selection for partial payment runs — the core bottleneck. They were never designed to solve the problems that consume the most AP team time.

BILL & Stampli

Cover Stages 1 and 3–6, with Stage 2 (approval/procurement depth) as an open question for demos. If their approval capabilities are strong enough, they could potentially serve as the client-facing layer too. If not, they sit alongside the existing procurement systems and handle everything downstream.

Basis

Goes deepest at Stage 3 — contextual AI coding with confidence scoring — and has its own intake layer (Stage 1) that connects to existing document sources. But it doesn't cover payment selection, payment execution, or procurement workflows.

What the stack actually looks like if Charter Impact adds an AP platform

The coverage map shows that BILL and Stampli address Stages 3–6 — the stages where the biggest pain points live. But Charter Impact's clients aren't all on the same system today, and they won't all be tomorrow. The practical question is: for each type of client, what changes and what stays?

This doesn't have to be all-or-nothing. The realistic outcome is that most clients move to the new AP platform, but a subset stays on their current system where that makes sense.

Clients currently on Sage only — the majority

The simplest case. Today, these clients submit invoices via ShareFile, and Charter Impact codes and pays everything directly in Sage. Adding an AP platform means invoices flow through BILL or Stampli instead — better coding intelligence at Stage 3, a real payment selection workflow at Stage 4, automated payment execution at Stage 5, and bidirectional sync back to Sage at Stage 6. The AP platform sits on top of Sage. No system is being replaced — it's a net addition that addresses the core pain points.

Stack: Sage + BILL/Stampli

Clients currently on Edstruments — ~8 clients

Edstruments' primary value is approval workflows (Stage 2) and a coding interface the AP team finds easier to use than Sage. But it has no payment status sync-back to Sage — associates manually update Edstruments after every payment run. Its payment capabilities (via Deluxe) have had a checkered history. And its coding intelligence is basic OCR without confidence scoring.

If BILL or Stampli's approval workflows can match what Edstruments provides — department-based routing, resource code routing, multi-level approvals — then there's a clear path to replacing Edstruments entirely. The AP team gets a single system for Stages 1–6 instead of shuttling between Edstruments and Sage. The manual payment-status sync-back problem goes away. The partnership consideration is lower here — Edstruments is not a significant driver of new business for Charter Impact.

This is a demo question: can the AP platform's approval workflows satisfy what Edstruments clients need? If yes, these clients move. If not, Edstruments stays for this subset.

Likely stack: Sage + BILL/Stampli (replacing Edstruments)

Clients currently on Procurify — ~7 clients, 3–4 more onboarding

Procurify is the harder case, for two reasons. First, Procurify has a capability that no AP automation platform is likely to match: PO punch-outs with direct integrations to Amazon Business, Staples, Home Depot, Grainger, Uline, and others. For clients that use punch-outs heavily, that's a real feature they'd lose. Second, Procurify is a meaningful sales and referral partner for Charter Impact — the partnership drives significant deal flow.

Running Procurify and an AP platform like BILL or Stampli for the same client doesn't make practical sense. Both systems would be trying to handle invoicing, coding, and payments — overlapping at Stages 3–5 with different integration paths to Sage. That creates more complexity, not less.

The practical approach: keep Procurify for clients that need it — particularly those with heavy punch-out usage and complex PO workflows — and keep offering it to new clients where its procurement depth is the right fit. For the majority of Charter Impact's client base that doesn't need those features, the AP platform becomes the default path. This preserves the partnership while still giving Charter Impact the benefits of AP automation for most of its portfolio.

The trade-off is real: Procurify clients won't get the coding intelligence, payment selection, or payment execution improvements. But that's a manageable subset — roughly 10 clients out of 80+ — and it's the cost of preserving a partnership that has value.

Stack: Sage + Procurify (no change for these clients)

New clients going forward

Charter Impact drives system selection for new clients. With an AP platform in place, the default recommendation for most new clients would be BILL or Stampli — it covers approval workflows, coding, and payments in a single system on top of Sage. For clients that specifically need Procurify's procurement depth (punch-outs, complex PO lifecycle, supplier catalog integrations), Procurify remains an option. This is the optionality framing: Charter Impact offers the right system per client, not one system for everyone.

Default: Sage + BILL/Stampli Where needed: Sage + Procurify

What this means in practice

Roughly 80% or more of Charter Impact's client base would be on the AP platform — all current Sage-only clients, all or most Edstruments clients, and all new clients that don't need Procurify's procurement features. The remaining ~10–15% stay on Procurify. The AP team would primarily work in one system (AP platform + Sage) with a known subset of clients on a different workflow (Procurify + Sage).

This is a significant improvement over today, where every client can be on a different system and associates describe onboarding to a new client as "almost like working for a different company." Going from five or six systems to two primary paths is a meaningful simplification — even if it's not full consolidation down to one.
